2 /* ++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++
3  File lusol2 LUSOL heap management routines
4  Hbuild Hchange Hdelete Hdown Hinsert Hup
5  Heap-management routines for LUSOL's lu1fac.
6  May be useful for other applications.
7  ++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++
8  For LUSOL, the heap structure involves three arrays of length N.
9  N is the current number of entries in the heap.
10  Ha(1:N) contains the values that the heap is partially sorting.
11  For LUSOL they are double precision values -- the largest
12  element in each remaining column of the updated matrix.
13  The biggest entry is in Ha(1), the top of the heap.
14  Hj(1:N) contains column numbers j.
15  Ha(k) is the biggest entry in column j = Hj(k).
16  Hk(1:N) contains indices within the heap. It is the
17  inverse of Hj(1:N), so k = Hk(j) <=> j = Hj(k).
18  Column j is entry k in the heap.
19  hops is the number of heap operations,
20  i.e., the number of times an entry is moved
21  (the number of "hops" up or down the heap).
22  Together, Hj and Hk let us find values inside the heap
23  whenever we want to change one of the values in Ha.
24  For other applications, Ha may need to be some other data type,
25  like the keys that sort routines operate on.
26  ++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++
27  11 Feb 2002: MATLAB version derived from "Algorithms" by
28  R. Sedgewick
29  03 Mar 2002: F77 version derived from MATLAB version.
30  07 May 2002: Safeguard input parameters k, N, Nk.
31  We don't want them to be output!
32  07 May 2002: Current version of lusol2.f.
33  ++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++ */
